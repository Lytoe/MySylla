Univeristies after LLM era

2025-10-20 · university - 2025

So, yeah… it’s 2025 and here I am, moved all the way to France just for the chance to sit in a classroom and watch a professor go through PowerPoint slides for six hours a week. Dream come true? Nah—not exactly. It’s real life, and honestly, it’s not even a joke. Anyway, what I'm about to share is just my own experience—not the entire story for everyone. I’m super curious about this: these days, you can pick up practically any skill you want, whether it’s quantum mechanics or learning the piano, just by grinding away online. If you’re dedicated, there’s a clear path. But here's the thing I keep noticing. The industry doesn’t just hand out jobs because you have a degree anymore. Companies interview, filter, and choose people based on what they can actually do. Yet universities are supposed to prepare us for those roles… but with all the new tech, LLMs, GPTs and whatnot, it’s getting harder for schools to really prepare students. The crazy part? Studying itself is easier than ever, so most students aren’t really pushing themselves to build the skills they need. They graduate, apply for jobs—and are totally unprepared for what comes next.
